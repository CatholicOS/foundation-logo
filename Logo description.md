## **Logo Description**

### **1\. Composition & Geometry**

A circular emblem (roundel) consisting of a primary background disc. Centered within this circle are two main layers:

* **Background Layer:** A stylized representation of a **global network**. This is composed of multiple intersecting arcs and latitude/longitude-style lines that form a sphere. The intersection points are marked by small, solid circular nodes, evoking the imagery of a digital network, data nodes, or a connected global community.  
* **Foreground Layer:** A bold, **white Latin cross** superimposed directly over the center of the globe. The cross is clean and proportional, with straight edges and no serifs.

### **2\. Color Palette**

The logo utilizes a tri-color scheme:

* **Navy Blue (Background):** A deep, dark blue that fills the “earth globe” and represents the sea, symbolizing longevity, life, and humanity.  
* **Gold/Ochre (Network Lines):** The network grid and nodes are rendered in a muted gold or metallic ochre, symbolizing value, light, and connectivity. This net symbolizes a fisherman’s net, the “internet”, and a “neural network”.  
* **White (The Cross):** A stark, solid white that provides high contrast and serves as the primary focal point.

---

### **3\. Symbolic Interpretation (for Trademark Context)**

When filing for a trademark, it is often helpful to define the "intended meaning" to protect the brand's conceptual space:

* **The Globe/Network:** Represents the "Digitalis" aspect—global reach, digital connectivity, and the modern internet era.  
* **The Cross:** Represents the "Missio" aspect—faith, Christian mission, and the core religious identity of the organization.  
* **The Synergy:** The placement of the cross over the network suggests the integration of faith into the digital landscape or a mission that spans the entire connected world.

### **5\. Concept and Symbolism of the Logo**

* **The Cross**  
  The foundational concept for the logo centers on the enduring sovereignty of Christ, visually represented by the **Cross of Christ triumphantly superimposed upon a Globe**. This core imagery immediately declares the theme: **Christ is King**, signifying His universal dominion and the global mission of the association. The **Cross of Christ** is the ultimate symbol of God's sacrificial love and the instrument of humanity's redemption. Its placement over the globe signifies that Christ’s Kingship is not limited to a single nation, people, or era, but is universal and absolute. It asserts that the authority of Christ, established through his death and resurrection, extends over the **entire world**—over all creation, history, and humanity.  
* **The Globe**  
  The CDCF’s international character is signified by the **Globe**, which represents the totality of the cosmos, the world in its geographical and temporal fullness. By crowning it with the Cross, the logo declares that Christ’s Lordship is the ultimate reality and the central principle by which the world is governed. It is an image that calls the viewer to recognize Jesus Christ as the **Sovereign Ruler** of all things, whose Kingdom transcends all earthly boundaries and political structures. The **Globe** underscores the international scope, clearly conveying that the CDCF is not confined to any one region but is inherently **international** in its membership, vision, and impact.  
* **The Net**  
  This foundational image is overlaid with a crucial element: a **Net**. The net serves a rich, tripartite symbolic function:  
1. **Biblical Mandate (Evangelization):** Primarily, the net is a direct reference to the Gospel commission, echoing Christ's call to His disciples: "I will make of you **fishers of men**" (Matthew 4:19). This symbolizes the core mission of worldwide evangelization and reaching souls for Christ.  
2. **Global Connectivity (Internet and Communication):** In a contemporary context, the net represents the **"internet"**—the ubiquitous digital framework that defines our **globally connected world**. This acknowledges the vital role of modern communication and technology in facilitating global ministry and outreach.  
3. **Technological Engagement (AI and Neural Networks):** Finally, the interwoven structure of the net, forming a highly interconnected **network** of nodes and connections, speaks directly to the realm of Artificial Intelligence. It evokes the concepts of **"neural networks"**—the computational backbone of modern AI—and the complex, interconnected systems of the digital space. This element signals the association's intention to engage thoughtfully and ethically with the technological frontier, including the AI space, as a platform for mission and dialogue.
